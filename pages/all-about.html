  • Species and Classification
    Dik-diks are part of the *genus Madoqua*, with four main species: Kirk’s dik-dik, Günther’s dik-dik, Silver dik-dik, and Salt’s dik-dik.
  • Size and Weight
    Adults are 30–40 cm tall at the shoulder and weigh 3–6 kg — about the size of a house cat.
  • Lifespan
    In the wild, they live 4–5 years, but in captivity, they can live up to 10 years.
  • Habitat
    They live in arid regions across eastern and southern Africa, including savannas and bushlands.
  • Monogamous Behavior
    Dik-diks form lifelong monogamous pairs and share a small, defended territory for their whole lifetime.
  • Territorial Marking
    They use scent from preorbital glands to mark their territory by rubbing on twigs and grass to create boundaries that other dik-diks avoid.
  • Heat Adaptations
    Their long snouts cool blood through a network of nasal passages, helping them survive hot, dry climates.
  • Diet
    Dik-diks feed primarily on leaves, fruits, shoots, and herbs. They get most of their water from food and rarely need to drink.
  • Predators and Survival
    Their predators include eagles, leopards, jackals, and pythons. Dik-diks rely on speed, camouflage, and agility, and they escape threats by blending into the brush and staying alert.
  • Communication
    They make a distinctive alarm call — a sharp “zik-zik” sound — which is how they got their name. It serves as a warning to their mate or nearby dik-diks.
