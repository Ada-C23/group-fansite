Global Recipe Facts

Devil's Food Cake (USA) 🌎

With its devilishly dark color and ultra-moist crumb, this American classic is a chocoholic’s dream.

Did you know? The name "Devil’s Food" became popular in the early 1900s as a bold, rebellious alternative to light "Angel Food" cake.

Tres Leches Cake (Latin America) 🌎

A beloved sponge cake soaked in three kinds of milk, making it creamy and rich with every bite.

Fun Fact: This cake's recipe became widespread thanks to a canned milk campaign in Latin America by Nestlé during the mid-20th century.

Cușma lui Guguță (Moldova) 🌎

A playful dessert of cherry-filled crepes stacked like a hat, named after a popular Moldovan storybook character.

Did you know? This dessert is named after Guguță — a boy whose magical hat grows as he offers warmth to others, making it a symbol of generosity and folklore.

Napoleon Cake (Eurasia) 🌎

A multi-layered cake with puff pastry and custard, this treat is a staple of Eurasia holidays and celebrations.

Fun Fact: Created in 1912 to honor the victory over Napoleon, each delicate layer represents the strength and endurance of tradition.
