Pizza History

  • Ancient Roots: Flatbreads with toppings existed in ancient civilizations like Egypt, Greece, and Rome.
  • Naples Origins: Modern pizza traces its beginnings to Naples, Italy, where in the 16th century, "pizza" referred to various flatbreads. The introduction of the tomato was a game-changer.
  • Margherita Legend (1889): The famous story involves Queen Margherita's visit to Naples and a pizza with tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil created in her honor.
  • First Pizzerias (19th Century): Naples saw the opening of the earliest pizzerias, like Antica Pizzeria Port'Alba (1830).
  • Arrival in America (Late 19th - Early 20th Century): Italian immigrants brought pizza to the U.S., with Lombardi's in NYC (1905) being the first licensed pizzeria.
  • Post-WWII Boom: American soldiers returning from Italy popularized pizza across the United States, leading to regional styles.
